Who We Are

Egypt's first STEM-focused public high school for girls, nurturing innovation leaders since 2012

Our Story

Pioneering excellence in girls' STEM education since 2012

Established in 2012 through a historic collaboration between USAID, World Learning, and the Egyptian Ministry of Education, Maadi STEM School for Girls stands as Egypt's first STEM-focused public high school for young women.

Our mission is to foster innovation, critical thinking, and leadership among Egypt's brightest female students, preparing them to become the next generation of scientists, engineers, and technology leaders.

"We see our impact in the story of a young woman named Nourhan in Egypt... when Nourhan got accepted to girls science and technology boarding school... today she is an avid coder and when speaking about her plans in the future she said I dream of being the youngest Nobel prize winner for nuclear physics."

- Michelle Obama, former First Lady of the United States

As a 3-year, public high school enrolling 340 students, we foster a hands-on learning approach where students take the lead in preparing and explaining topics collaboratively and independently. Our residential campus creates a tight-knit community that extends beyond the classroom.

Our Educational Philosophy

Innovative approaches that prepare students for tomorrow's challenges

Harkness Pedagogy

Student-led discussions and collaborative learning where teachers facilitate rather than direct. Small class sizes of 24 students foster meaningful engagement.

Project-Based Learning

Real-world problem solving through semester-long capstone projects addressing Egypt's Grand Challenges using the Engineering Design Process.

Hands-On Experience

Practical laboratory work, prototype development, and experimental design in 9 specialized labs including Fabrication, Computer, and Multimedia labs.
